Indoor air quality testing in the modern age.

What is IAQ?

Indoor Air Quality (IAQ) testing is an environmental testing process that aims to measure the amount of potentially detrimental contaminants. The measurements taken often include Carbon Dioxide (CO2), Carbon Monoxide (CO), Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs), 2.5 micron diameter particulates (PM2.5), or 10 micron diameter particulates (PM10). Introduction

Indoor Air Quality (IAQ) testing is a frequently run test executed by environmental engineering to verify healthy air quality in high-risk buildings. Mid-size firms in the Chicago area execute 400+ tests annually, each one taking 5+ man hours. Hundreds of thousands of people rely on these tests to mitigate air related health issues.

I am developing IAQuick as an accessible, optimized solution to old processes in the environmental engineering industry. The solution aims to revolutionize the IAQ testing process by enabling surveyors to record, process, format, and export air quality readings in a streamlined process. IAQuick is increasing time efficiency of IAQ testing by over 85%.

Background

In 2021 I interned at Specialty Consulting, Inc. an environmental engineering company that executes IAQ tests over 400 times per year. During my internship I worked with the IAQ team for the summer in a variety of responsibilities. This gave me insight to the opportunity for an increase in efficiency.

Problem

Each survey that went through the company had a multi-step process.

Step Description Time (hrs)
1 Collecting survey data on paper in the field 1
2 Scanning each survey in the office .5
3 Sending the images to another team member .5
4 Transcription of the image to an Excel sheet 1
5 Quality check and export to PDF .5
6 Writing of letter to site admin team .5
7 Submission of results to administrators .5
4.5 hrs

Proposition

The process as laid above, has many potential areas for inefficiency and human error. I aimed to improve a process that took nearly 1800 hours annually (4.5 hours * 200 sites * 2 times annually). The IAQuick app is designed to reduce the previous process by merging and avoiding various steps.

New Process

Step Description Time (hrs)
1 Data input to IAQuick app in the field .5
2 Local & editable data storage of survey input 0
3 Export PDF to email platform, ready to send .1
.6 hrs

Technical Journey

Development Framework

Flutter was chosen as the development framework based on its features including:

  • Ability to maintain a single codebase across platforms.
  • Its high performance on both iOS and Android.
  • Rich widget library.
  • Hot reload functionality for rapid development.

Development Methodology

This application is being developed using an MVP architecture to ensure quicker time to market for specific applications, market validation for future growth, and enable me to continuously ask the team at Specialty Consulting for feedback.

Data Storage

After experimentation with CSV storage, Google Firestore, and direct storage in Excel Sheets, SQLite was determined to be the ideal database. The platform's flexibility in design coupled with my previous experience, and efficiency at size made it a logical choice.

Challenges

This development process gave me the opportunity to resolve a wider variety of issues from practical issues regarding the end-user workflow, issues with data storage, add using logins, etc. Main issues included:

  • Network instability at work sites resolved by conversion to fully offline process
  • Data export and formatting issues that are in the process of being resolved by alternative data processing streams.

Future

The next phase of this project involves ironing out data storage nuances in testing phases and then moving to pilot deployment with Specialty Consulting.

In the long term, this app will grow to be capable of handling many types of testing outside of IAQ.
